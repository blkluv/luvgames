# Introduction

Hello content writers, if you need to use math in your blog posts, you can read this for help. (the 1 is a footnote)
^[TeX functions can be found in the [KaTeX documentation](https://katex.org/docs/supported.html)]

Inline math symbols can be included by enclosing the term between the `$` symbol.

Math code blocks are denoted by `$$`.

### Examples of math notation you can achieve

$$
\mathbf{Y} = \left[\begin{array}
  {c}
  y_1 \\
  . \\
  . \\
  . \\
  y_n
\end{array}\right]
$$

$$
\mathbf{X} = \left[\begin{array}
  {ccccc}
  x_{11} & . & . & . & x_{1k} \\
  . & . & . & . & .  \\
  . & . & . & . & .  \\
  . & . & . & . & .  \\
  x_{n1} & . & . & . & x_{nn}
\end{array}\right] =
\left[\begin{array}
  {c}
  \mathbf{x}'_1 \\
  . \\
  . \\
  . \\
  \mathbf{x}'_n
\end{array}\right]
$$

$$
Q = \sum_{i=1}^{n}{u_i^2} = \sum_{i=1}^{n}{(y_i - \mathbf{x}'_i\beta)^2} = (Y-X\beta)'(Y-X\beta)
$$
